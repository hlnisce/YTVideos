Once upon a time, a sweet girl named Little Red Riding Hood lived in a cozy cottage at the edge of a vast forest.
She was called by this name because she always wore a bright red velvet cloak that her grandmother had made for her.
One sunny morning, Mother called Little Red Riding Hood into the kitchen to prepare a very special delivery.
Mother placed a fresh loaf of bread and a jar of sweet honey into a sturdy wicker basket.
Grandmother is feeling a bit poorly today, so I would like you to take these treats to her house, Mother said.
Little Red Riding Hood put on her favorite red cloak and promised to be very careful on her journey.
Remember, stay on the path and do not speak to any strangers you might meet in the woods, Mother warned.
Little Red Riding Hood waved goodbye and stepped out into the bright, warm sunshine of the morning.
As she entered the forest, the tall trees whispered in the wind and the air smelled of pine and wildflowers.
She skipped along the winding dirt path, watching colorful butterflies flutter through the dappled light.
Deep in the shadows of the woods, Wolf, a large predatory canine with thick grey fur, was watching her.
Wolf stepped out from behind a massive oak tree and greeted Little Red Riding Hood with a low, smooth voice.
Good morning, Little Red Riding Hood, and where are you going on this fine, beautiful day, Wolf asked.
Forgetting Mother’s warning, Little Red Riding Hood stopped to tell Wolf all about her sick grandmother.
She lives in the little cottage under the three big oak trees near the sparkling river, Little Red Riding Hood explained.
The cunning Wolf hatched a wicked plan and suggested that the girl should pick some flowers for Grandmother.
While Little Red Riding Hood wandered off the path to find yellow daisies, Wolf took a secret shortcut.
Wolf ran as fast as his powerful legs could carry him, racing toward Grandmother’s house.
Wolf arrived at the cottage and knocked softly on the wooden door with his heavy, furry paw.
Grandmother, thinking it was her granddaughter, told the visitor to come inside and rest.
Wolf burst into the room and, before the poor woman could move, he jumped toward Grandmother.
Wolf then put on a lacy nightcap and round glasses before climbing into the bed and pulling up the covers.
Soon, Little Red Riding Hood reached the cottage and noticed that the front door was standing wide open.
She stepped inside the quiet house and felt a strange chill as she approached Grandmother’s bed.
Oh, Grandmother, what big ears you have, Little Red Riding Hood whispered, looking at the strange figure.
All the better to hear you with, my dear, Wolf replied in a high, squeaky voice.
But Grandmother, what big eyes you have, Little Red Riding Hood said, leaning closer to the glowing amber orbs.
All the better to see you with, my dear, Wolf growled with a hungry, toothy grin.
And Grandmother, what big teeth you have, Little Red Riding Hood cried out as the figure suddenly sat up.
All the better to eat you with, Wolf roared, lunging forward just as Woodcutter burst through the door.
