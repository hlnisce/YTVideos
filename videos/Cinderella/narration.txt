Once upon a time, in a faraway kingdom, there lived a kind and beautiful girl named Cinderella.

She lived with her cruel stepmother and two jealous stepsisters who made her do all the housework.

Every morning Cinderella swept the floors, washed the dishes, and cooked meals for her ungrateful family.

Her stepsisters wore fine gowns while Cinderella wore a tattered old dress and wooden shoes.

At night, Cinderella would sit by the fireplace and talk to her little mouse friends who lived in the walls.

One day, an invitation arrived from the royal palace announcing a grand ball.

The King wanted his son, the Prince, to find a bride at the wonderful celebration.

Cinderella's stepmother smiled wickedly and said only her two daughters could attend the ball.

Cinderella pleaded to go, but her stepmother gave her even more chores to keep her busy.

The stepsisters spent all day trying on sparkly dresses and fixing their hair for the ball.

When the night of the ball arrived, Cinderella watched from the window as her family left in a carriage.

A single tear rolled down her cheek as she sat alone in the cold dark kitchen.

Suddenly, a bright golden light filled the room, and a kind Fairy Godmother appeared.

She waved her magic wand and said, "Dry your tears, dear child, you shall go to the ball."

The Fairy Godmother turned a pumpkin into a beautiful golden carriage with her magic.

She transformed four little mice into magnificent white horses to pull the carriage.

Then she waved her wand over Cinderella and a shimmering blue gown appeared on her delicate body.

Sparkling glass slippers appeared on Cinderella's tiny feet, catching the moonlight like diamonds.

The Fairy Godmother warned Cinderella that the magic would end at midnight.

Cinderella climbed into the golden carriage and rode off toward the glowing palace.

When she entered the ballroom, the entire hall fell silent as everyone gazed at her beauty.

The Prince walked slowly toward her and gently asked her to dance.

They twirled and glided across the sparkling floor, falling in love with every step.

The jealous stepsisters watched from the corner, not recognizing the mysterious princess.

Hours passed like minutes as the enchanted couple danced under the glittering chandeliers.

Then the great clock began to strike midnight, and Cinderella gasped in fright.

She ran down the grand staircase, losing one glass slipper on the cold stone steps.

Cinderella jumped into the carriage just as the magic faded, returning everything to normal.

The next day, the Prince searched the entire kingdom with the tiny glass slipper.

Finally he found Cinderella, the slipper fit perfectly, and they lived happily ever after.
